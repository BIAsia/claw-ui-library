# Agent Probe / Case 021 / Cygnito Mono Poster — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 021 should become a monochrome systems bulletin: a severe, type-led announcement page for a software release, protocol gathering, writer-developer salon, or machine-poetry publication rather than a warm editorial feature.**

This is a disciplined mono broadside.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title names **mono poster**, implying single-family type discipline and reduced color complexity.
- “Cygnito” suggests a synthetic, coded, or futuristic identity that suits a systems-oriented bulletin.
- Poster framing means the product should behave as a **public announcement sheet**, not an application UI.
- Monospaced typography naturally supports **technical precision and serialized metadata**.
- The likely effect depends on restraint, repetition, and exact alignment rather than imagery.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is best for:
- protocol or software release notices
- technical reading series posters
- machine literature publications
- experimental conferences
- serialized issue announcements

It is good at:
- severity
- precision
- coded atmosphere
- typographic structure

It is bad for:
- emotional consumer branding
- dense browsing interfaces
- colorful campaigns
- image-led storytelling

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Monospaced typographic discipline must be preserved.** The voice comes from uniform letter rhythm and rigid alignment.
2. **The palette should remain tightly restrained.** Black/white or near-monochrome contrast is central to the family.
3. **Poster structure must remain visible.** Think fixed bands, blocks, serial lines, or bulletin compartments.
4. **Metadata should feel coded.** Use issue numbers, timestamps, coordinates, version strings, or compact factual lines.
5. **The page must avoid warmth and softness.** Its power comes from formal control.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Protocol Reading No. 7** — a monochrome poster announcing a writer-developer salon about machine language, code form, and synthetic literature.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The first read is **strict typographic bulletin**.
- Monospace treatment shapes the entire page identity.
- Color use remains minimal and disciplined.
- Metadata lines feel serial and intentional.
- The page feels public-facing, not app-like.
- The concept plausibly fits a technical-cultural gathering or release.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the design becomes stylish minimalism without coded rigor.
- **Fail if** warm imagery or decorative gradients soften the mode.
- **Fail if** proportional type takes over the hierarchy.

- **Pass if** the poster feels austere, exact, and systems-minded.
- **Pass if** mono typography is doing the heavy expressive work.
- **Pass if** the concept reads like a serious bulletin for a technical-cultural audience.
