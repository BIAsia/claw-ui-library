# Agent Probe / Case 016 / Cashless Adoption Dots — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 016 should become a pattern-and-coverage explainer: a visual adoption map for payments, service uptake, network rollout, or behavioral change where dotted distribution is the core grammar rather than traditional charts.**

This is a spread-of-adoption surface.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title implies **adoption represented through dots**, pointing toward distributed coverage rather than singular metrics.
- Cashless systems often need to show **growth, penetration, participation, and category spread**.
- Dots naturally express **many small units becoming a visible pattern**.
- The likely wide format supports an infographic-like explanation board.
- This mode is better for **narrating systemic uptake** than for granular operations dashboards.

---

## Style-family thesis
Use this family for:
- adoption explainers
- network rollout visuals
- category penetration maps
- public policy uptake stories
- service access overviews
- trend communication pages

It is good at:
- showing scale through repetition
- making abstract adoption feel tangible
- balancing data and design

It is bad for:
- precise analytical drilling
- archives and search
- high-text narratives
- campaign posters without data logic

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Dots or repeated micro-units must be the dominant visual device.** They should represent participation, spread, or density directly.
2. **The pattern must encode meaning.** Cluster, quantity, color, or motion should reveal adoption logic, not just decorate the page.
3. **A concise narrative frame must explain what is spreading.** One strong headline and short subheads are enough.
4. **The composition should feel infographic-clean.** Avoid cluttered dashboard widgets and excessive chrome.
5. **Macro insight must beat precision.** The user should first understand the shape of change, then any exact figures.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Tap Shift Map** — a public explainer for transit card and mobile payment adoption across neighborhoods, using dot fields to show participation growth over time.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as an **adoption pattern explainer**.
- Dot repetition is the main communication device.
- The pattern clearly means something measurable.
- A viewer can infer the overall trend quickly.
- The interface feels more like designed information than dashboard plumbing.
- The translated concept plausibly explains a real rollout or uptake story.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the dots are merely decorative texture.
- **Fail if** the page turns into a conventional metrics dashboard.
- **Fail if** the narrative frame is too weak to explain the pattern.

- **Pass if** the distribution itself teaches the viewer about adoption.
- **Pass if** the page feels like a crisp public-facing systems explainer.
- **Pass if** the macro pattern remains legible even before exact values are read.
