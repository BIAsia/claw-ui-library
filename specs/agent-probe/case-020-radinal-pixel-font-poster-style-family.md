# Agent Probe / Case 020 / Radinal Pixel Font Poster — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 020 should become a digital-publication poster: a typographic announcement surface for game jams, digital art drops, code-poetry releases, or experimental software editions rather than a standard brand page.**

This is a bitmap declaration object.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title highlights **pixel font**, making typography itself the primary visual event.
- Poster framing suggests a **public-facing announcement sheet** rather than app UI.
- Pixel type carries associations with **digital craft, retro computation, and low-resolution intentionality**.
- The likely composition depends on **large type, modular spacing, and graphic bluntness**.
- This style works best when the concept can embrace **digital artifact energy**.

---

## Style-family thesis
Use this family for:
- digital release posters
- game or code event announcements
- experimental software editions
- internet-culture bulletins
- pixel-art festival notices

It is good at:
- typographic character
- graphic bluntness
- digital nostalgia
- public announcement clarity

It is bad for:
- nuanced editorial reading
- enterprise credibility pages
- dense navigation systems
- soft lifestyle branding

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Pixel or bitmap typography must dominate the identity.** This is not optional ornament; it is the core mode.
2. **The poster must preserve chunky digital rhythm.** Spacing, alignment, and blocks should feel discrete and snapped.
3. **Content should stay concise and announcement-shaped.** Title, date, version, host, and short subtitle are enough.
4. **The page should feel like a designed digital artifact.** Avoid smoothing it into clean modern minimalism.
5. **Graphic contrast must remain bold.** The style loses force if everything becomes subtle.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Patch Notes Live 01** — a poster for an experimental code-art showcase where artists present tiny software pieces, patches, and playable poems.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **typographic digital poster**.
- Pixel typography is unmistakably central.
- The content is brief and public-facing.
- The design retains crisp, snapped, blocky rhythm.
- The concept plausibly belongs to digital culture.
- The page would still work as a printed or shared announcement object.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** pixel type is reduced to a decorative accent.
- **Fail if** the poster becomes a generic landing page with retro styling.
- **Fail if** smoothing and polish erase the bitmap character.

- **Pass if** the typography alone identifies the family instantly.
- **Pass if** the page feels like a genuine digital-publication artifact.
- **Pass if** the concept suits code, games, or experimental internet culture.
