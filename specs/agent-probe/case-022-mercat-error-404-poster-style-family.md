# Agent Probe / Case 022 / Mercat Error 404 Poster — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 022 should become a branded exception page poster: a high-character failure surface for unavailable routes, closed exhibitions, sold-out drops, or suspended catalog items where the “error” itself becomes a designed public artifact rather than a dead-end utility page.**

This is an expressive failure state.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title explicitly names **Error 404 Poster**, which makes posterized failure the central idea.
- “Mercat” hints at a market, storefront, or public-programme context where unavailable states can still carry brand value.
- A poster treatment lets the product turn a problem into a **memorable public sign**.
- This mode works best when absence, closure, or misdirection is **acknowledged with design confidence**.
- The source likely favors one dominant error declaration plus a small recovery path.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is ideal for:
- expressive 404 pages
- sold-out notices
- archive-unavailable states
- ticket closure pages
- expired campaign pages
- maintenance broadside states

It is good at:
- making failure memorable
- preserving brand tone in broken states
- concise recovery messaging

It is bad for:
- normal content pages
- dense help documentation
- application dashboards
- high-volume browsing

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **The missing-state declaration must dominate.** 404, unavailable, closed, expired, or lost should be visually unavoidable.
2. **Poster logic must remain intact.** The page should still feel like a designed placard or broadside, not a template utility screen.
3. **Recovery paths must be sparse and clear.** One or two next actions are enough.
4. **Tone should be clever or branded, but never confusing.** The user should still understand what happened.
5. **The absence itself should feel intentional.** Empty space, oversized type, or graphic interruption should reinforce the state.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Closed Stall Notice** — an art-market archive page for a sold-out edition that turns the unavailable state into a branded poster with archive return and waitlist links.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **designed error / unavailable poster**.
- The failure message is immediately clear.
- The page retains strong brand character despite minimal content.
- Recovery options are visible and few.
- Empty or interrupted space contributes to meaning.
- The concept plausibly belongs to a cultural commerce or public-programme context.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page feels like a standard framework 404 template.
- **Fail if** style overwhelms basic comprehension.
- **Fail if** recovery links are missing or buried.

- **Pass if** the error state feels like a deliberate poster in the product family.
- **Pass if** the user instantly grasps both the problem and next step.
- **Pass if** the page turns “missing” into a branded experience rather than an accident.
