# Agent Probe / Case 002 / I’m With Palestine — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 002 should be treated as a solidarity campaign broadside: a public support statement designed for advocacy, coalition publishing, fundraiser amplification, or awareness mobilization rather than a content browser or product homepage.**

This is a stance-taking surface.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title is explicitly political and declarative, which points toward **campaign communication** rather than neutral software UI.
- The likely poster-like framing suggests a **shareable public statement object**.
- The emotional center is alignment and visibility: **who stands where, and with whom**.
- A solidarity page benefits from **high contrast, slogan clarity, and minimal ambiguity**, all consistent with poster logic.
- The source name implies **urgency and public readability**, not feature explanation.

---

## Style-family thesis
This style family is for products that need to publish:
- solidarity statements
- cause campaigns
- public sign-on pages
- advocacy bulletins
- fundraiser calls
- coalition launch pages

It is strong when the product must feel:
- principled
- public
- urgent
- shareable
- unmistakably aligned

It is weak for:
- neutral utility tools
- nuanced multi-step workflows
- dense archival browsing
- playful product marketing

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **The core slogan must be immediate.** The main declaration should dominate the page and remain readable at poster distance.
2. **The page must preserve protest-poster energy.** Do not over-soften it into corporate campaign polish.
3. **Supporting content must be action-shaped.** Use compact blocks for donate, read, sign, attend, or amplify — not long descriptive sections.
4. **Typography and contrast must carry conviction.** The design should feel bold enough to support a cause, not decorative for its own sake.
5. **The composition must stay singular and public-facing.** No dashboards, multi-column app chrome, or card-grid detours.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Stand Together Bulletin** — a coalition action page for a humanitarian relief network that publishes one public solidarity statement, one urgent update line, and three clear actions: donate, share resources, attend local event.

The style translation should preserve activist clarity over polished conversion-optimization tropes.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The first read is **solidarity / campaign statement**, not ordinary brand marketing.
- One declarative message governs the hierarchy.
- Supporting actions are compact and legible.
- The page feels public and urgent rather than corporate.
- The structure can plausibly be screenshotted and shared as a statement object.
- The implementation maintains moral seriousness without becoming visually muddy.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page reads like a startup homepage borrowing protest language.
- **Fail if** the main declaration is reduced to a subheading under product copy.
- **Fail if** excessive components dilute the urgency.

- **Pass if** the page feels like a cause-led broadside with clear public alignment.
- **Pass if** the primary statement is the unmistakable center of gravity.
- **Pass if** the supporting actions feel like campaign utilities, not growth hacks.
