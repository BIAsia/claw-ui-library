# Agent Probe / Case 014 / Marketing Tag Cluster — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 014 should become a message-mapping board: a campaign strategy surface for positioning themes, audience hooks, and offer variants where clusters of tags are the primary visual system rather than prose documents or funnel dashboards.**

This is a language-clustering tool.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title points to **tag clusters**, suggesting modular language units rather than fixed cards.
- Marketing strategy often lives in **themes, claims, personas, and hooks**, which cluster structures express well.
- The source likely values **density, association, and grouping** over strict rows and columns.
- Tag-based composition makes it easy to show **overlap, hierarchy, and message families**.
- The case feels more like a strategic wall than a metrics console.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family works for:
- messaging systems
- positioning workshops
- campaign theme boards
- naming explorations
- content angle mapping
- persona-language clustering

It is good at:
- association
- semantic grouping
- compact ideation
- showing relatedness visually

It is bad for:
- narrative reading
- scheduling
- finance or KPI reporting
- formal document presentation

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Tag or chip clusters must carry the composition.** The page should be built from many compact labels, not long cards.
2. **Grouping must be visible spatially.** Proximity, color, or cluster containers should communicate meaning at a glance.
3. **The experience should feel exploratory but not chaotic.** There must be enough order that a strategist can actually use it.
4. **Labels should stay short and slogan-like.** One phrase per chip is the right scale.
5. **Metrics and analytics must remain secondary or absent.** This is about language architecture, not campaign reporting.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Message Atlas** — a strategy board that maps campaign hooks for a nonprofit product launch across audience segments, benefit claims, and emotional framings.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **message architecture board**.
- Tag clusters are the main visual units.
- Spatial grouping reveals semantic relationships.
- The density feels usable rather than messy.
- Labels are brief and memorable.
- The translated concept plausibly supports marketing or editorial strategy work.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the layout becomes a table or dashboard with tags as decoration.
- **Fail if** clusters do not communicate relationships.
- **Fail if** long paragraphs replace chip-scale language units.

- **Pass if** the user can quickly see message families and overlap.
- **Pass if** the composition feels like a working strategy wall.
- **Pass if** the tag system drives both the visuals and the product meaning.
