# Agent Probe / Case 015 / Impact Campaign Card — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 015 should become a donation or mission campaign focal card: a public-interest conversion surface centered on one emotionally weighted card object rather than a long nonprofit microsite.**

This is a concentrated call-to-support artifact.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title combines **impact**, **campaign**, and **card**, signaling a persuasive public-interest object.
- A square-ish reference suggests a **social-shareable campaign module**.
- Campaign cards work well when the product needs **clarity, urgency, and one primary action**.
- The source likely balances a strong message, a result cue, and an action path in a compact frame.
- This mode is more about **focus and emotional conversion** than broad site exploration.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is suitable for:
- fundraising cards
- petition spotlights
- cause-specific donation pages
- emergency appeal modules
- impact update cards

It is strong at:
- focus
- emotional concentration
- quick understanding
- action clarity

It is weak for:
- archives
- browsing many options
- tool workflows
- detailed annual reports

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **One campaign card must dominate the experience.** It should hold message, proof, and action together.
2. **The action path must be immediate.** Donate, sign, share, or volunteer should be visible without scrolling.
3. **Proof must stay concise.** One stat, one outcome line, or one campaign context block is enough.
4. **The tone must feel earnest, not performatively “impactful.”** Avoid startup-ized optimism that weakens seriousness.
5. **Peripheral content must remain minimal.** The card should do the heavy lifting.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Relief Now Card** — a humanitarian emergency appeal card that pairs one urgent need statement with one impact figure and one direct donate action.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **mission campaign conversion card**.
- One primary action is unmistakable.
- Emotional message and proof are both present but compact.
- The card feels self-contained and shareable.
- Supporting interface stays out of the way.
- The concept plausibly belongs to a real public-interest campaign.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the card is buried inside a normal landing page.
- **Fail if** too many calls to action compete for attention.
- **Fail if** the tone becomes generic startup marketing.

- **Pass if** one card clearly concentrates the campaign’s purpose.
- **Pass if** the user can understand need, impact, and action in seconds.
- **Pass if** the page feels suitable for a real donation or advocacy push.
