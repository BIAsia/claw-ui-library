# Agent Probe / Case 010 / Dev Agency Hero Layout — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 010 should be treated as a credibility-first studio introduction: a service-facing front page for a technical design/build studio, infrastructure partner, or specialist engineering practice rather than a feature marketplace or app dashboard.**

This is an expertise signal surface.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title points to a **hero layout**, which means brand positioning and credibility are central.
- “Dev agency” implies the page must balance **technical competence with client-facing clarity**.
- The source likely uses a strong opening block, concise service signals, and a controlled layout rhythm.
- This mode fits concepts where **trust, specialization, and contact readiness** matter more than product feature depth.
- The likely composition supports a **statement + proof snippets** pattern well.

---

## Style-family thesis
Use this family for:
- specialist agency fronts
- consultancy introductions
- technical partner studios
- productized service pages
- expert practice launch surfaces

It works when the product needs to feel:
- sharp
- competent
- deliberate
- modern without being fluffy

It is weak for:
- large content archives
- activist campaigns
- poster-driven public notices
- dense internal tools

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **A single strong hero statement must lead.** The first screen should clearly tell the user what the studio does and what kind of expertise it claims.
2. **Proof should appear in compact, well-structured fragments.** Client names, service categories, delivery stats, or project snippets should support the claim without taking over.
3. **The layout must feel intentional and premium.** Spacing, alignment, and component rhythm should project craft.
4. **Calls to action should stay confident and sparse.** One primary next step and one secondary path are enough.
5. **The visual language must stay technical, not whimsical.** Avoid playful clutter or trend-chasing ornament.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Assembly Works** — a specialist product-engineering studio homepage focused on prototyping internal tools and shipping branded interfaces for cultural and tech clients.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **specialist studio front**, not startup app marketing.
- One value proposition dominates the hero.
- Supporting proof is visible but compact.
- The page feels credible and technically literate.
- The user can identify a clear contact/engagement path quickly.
- The implementation preserves compositional discipline over novelty.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page fills with too many feature tiles or vague marketing slogans.
- **Fail if** the hero lacks a precise expertise claim.
- **Fail if** the design becomes playful enough to undermine competence.

- **Pass if** the page feels like a high-end technical practice introducing itself.
- **Pass if** proof fragments reinforce rather than distract from the main claim.
- **Pass if** a prospective client could understand the studio’s role within seconds.
