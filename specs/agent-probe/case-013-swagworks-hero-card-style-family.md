# Agent Probe / Case 013 / Swagworks Hero Card — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 013 should become a bold product-introduction card: a single-offer launch surface for a brand, creator tool, or membership product where one oversized card carries the whole pitch rather than a full multi-section site.**

This is a hero object, not a complete website shell.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title names a **hero card**, implying one dominant container rather than many equal sections.
- “Swagworks” suggests a high-attitude brand voice suited to a **showpiece module**.
- Card-led hero compositions work well when the concept needs to feel **tight, memorable, and punchy**.
- The likely layout favors **one compact proposition with strong visual framing**.
- This mode fits launch objects, limited drops, and focused offerings better than broad corporate pages.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is for:
- single-product launches
- membership intros
- creator tools
- limited drops
- spotlight offers
- experiment pages

It is good at:
- singular focus
- memorable presentation
- compact persuasion
- strong visual packaging

It is bad for:
- large information architectures
- archives and browsing
- timelines and planning tools
- multi-offer ecommerce grids

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **One oversized card must own the screen.** Other elements may support it, but the hero object is the system center.
2. **Inside the card, hierarchy must be sharp.** Product name, one-line pitch, and one or two supporting signals should be enough.
3. **The brand voice should feel confident and stylized.** A little attitude is correct; generic SaaS neutrality is not.
4. **Supporting UI outside the card must stay minimal.** Avoid building a full app shell around it.
5. **The surface should feel packaged.** Borders, framing, depth, or internal segmentation should make the card feel like a designed object.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Crew Pass** — a members-only creative toolkit launch page presented almost entirely through one oversized access card with tier, perks, and join action.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **single-offer spotlight**.
- One card clearly dominates the frame.
- The offer is understandable within the card itself.
- The voice feels branded and assertive.
- Peripheral UI stays restrained.
- The page could plausibly be shared as one memorable launch artifact.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the card becomes just one module among many equal sections.
- **Fail if** the brand voice softens into generic startup copy.
- **Fail if** the layout requires deep scrolling to understand the offer.

- **Pass if** one hero object carries almost the entire experience.
- **Pass if** the card feels like a designed package rather than a plain panel.
- **Pass if** the concept feels right for a bold, tightly scoped launch.
