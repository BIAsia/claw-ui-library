# Agent Probe / Case 027 / Gdansk Literature System — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 027 should become a literary programme system: a structured cultural platform for readings, authors, editions, and city-linked literature events where typographic order and institutional identity matter more than flashy interaction.**

This is a civic-literary system surface.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title combines a city identity with **literature system**, suggesting an institutional but culturally rich design language.
- “System” implies more than one poster or page: a repeatable framework for programs, authors, and editions.
- Literature products benefit from **typographic seriousness and editorial structure**.
- The likely composition balances cultural prestige with public usability.
- This mode suits a city programme or festival platform better than a general library search UI.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is appropriate for:
- literature festivals
- municipal reading programmes
- author series platforms
- public library cultural calendars
- publication systems tied to place identity

It is good at:
- institutional clarity
- editorial dignity
- structured cultural publishing
- repeatable program identity

It is bad for:
- startup-style growth pages
- dense asset browsers
- playful consumer product marketing
- live technical monitoring

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Typographic order must anchor the system.** Titles, author names, dates, and venue information should feel formally organized.
2. **City or institutional identity must be present but restrained.** It should feel civic-cultural, not tourist-brand flashy.
3. **The layout should support repeatable cultural programming.** Think modular listings, issue structures, event blocks, or edition logic.
4. **Visual tone must remain literary and editorial.** Avoid product-market fit energy.
5. **The page should feel extensible into a larger system.** Not just a one-off poster, but part of a family.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Gdansk Reading Season** — a city literature programme hub presenting author nights, translation workshops, and publication releases through a disciplined civic-editorial system.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **cultural literature system**, not generic event site.
- Typographic structure is strong and orderly.
- The civic/institutional mood feels credible.
- Program blocks suggest repeatability across a series.
- The design supports authors, dates, and venues clearly.
- The concept plausibly belongs to a city-backed literary platform.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page becomes a trendy creative-studio promo.
- **Fail if** literary seriousness is replaced by lifestyle branding.
- **Fail if** the system cannot plausibly scale to multiple programme pages.

- **Pass if** the design feels both institutional and culturally alive.
- **Pass if** typography does most of the identity work.
- **Pass if** a reviewer could imagine an entire festival or city reading season built from it.
