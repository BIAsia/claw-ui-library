# Case 019 — BBBank Finance Cockpit style family

## Positioning

**Case 019 works best as a personal finance cockpit: a high-density mobile dashboard for balances, limits, schedules, and account actions rather than a generic banking shell or broad SaaS admin UI.**

## Why this case is family-worthy

The original BBBank dashboard is not a normal banking landing page. Its value comes from a **compressed dashboard mosaic** where many money signals coexist at once:
- date / temporal orientation
- calendar cadence
- monthly budget and remaining balance
- statements and direct debits
- savings goal progress
- engagement / trend framing
- quick account actions
- type specimen / identity texture

That combination makes it reusable as a family for products that need a **single-screen operational read** of personal finance, household finance, or account posture.

## Mode lock

This family must remain:
- **phone-like / narrow-surface / dashboard-first**
- **high-density and panel-based**
- **product-operational rather than editorial or marketing-led**

It must not drift into:
- a generic app shell with sidebar + header + content area
- a standard SaaS admin dashboard
- a hero landing page for a fintech brand
- a sparse minimal wallet screen with only one or two headline metrics

## Non-negotiables

1. Preserve the **three-column stacked-card composition** from the original reference.
2. Preserve the **high-contrast black field + acidic accent panel palette**.
3. Preserve **information density**—many modules visible at once, each with a distinct operational job.
4. Preserve the **dashboard/product mood**: numbers, dates, controls, obligations, and money movement should dominate.
5. Motion must stay **additive and restrained**: count-ups, slider movement, tiny hover lift, subtle bar growth.

## Suitable concept translations

Good translations:
- household treasury cockpit
- family budgeting dashboard
- shared-home expense command surface
- member wallet / allowance planner
- cashflow readiness dashboard
- goals + obligations overview

Less suitable translations:
- CRM
- issue tracker
- editorial publication
- generic analytics admin
- broad multi-page banking website

## Visual rules

- Use **dense rounded modules** with strong internal hierarchy.
- Let **compressed display typography** drive the first impression.
- Keep the **mobile-dashboard feel** even on a larger page by centering a narrow multi-column composition.
- Accent modules should feel bold but purposeful, never decorative for their own sake.
- Retain the original mix of **date/calendar, money, list, and utility panels**.

## Page constraints

- The user should understand the product in one glance.
- Multiple financial jobs should happen at once on the page.
- Avoid extra framing chrome like desktop navigation rails or promotional footers.
- Avoid replacing the core dashboard with a single hero metric.

## First concept probe

### Household Treasury

Translate the original BBBank dashboard into a **shared-home finance cockpit**.

Key adaptation rules:
- keep the original module rhythm
- reinterpret copy around household budgets, upcoming bills, family reserve, and shared transfers
- preserve the branded/product nature of the source
- do not turn it into a lifestyle planner or consumer marketing page

## Evaluation checklist

- Does it still feel like case-019 within one second?
- Does it remain a finance cockpit rather than a template dashboard?
- Are the modules dense, legible, and operational?
- Are palette and typographic compression preserved?
- Does the concept plausibly support day-to-day money decisions?
