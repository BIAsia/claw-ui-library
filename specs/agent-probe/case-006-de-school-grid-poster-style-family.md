# Agent Probe / Case 006 / De School Grid Poster — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 006 should become a programme timetable poster: a schedule-first cultural listing surface for venues, festivals, workshops, or club programming rather than a narrative editorial or generic event landing page.**

This is a grid of programmed time.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title explicitly names a **grid poster**, which strongly suggests schedule logic over freeform storytelling.
- The tall poster ratio implies a **printed noticeboard artifact** rather than an app shell.
- “De School” cues a nightlife / cultural programme vibe where **date-time-location structure** matters.
- The likely composition depends on **strict columns and bands**, ideal for timetable information.
- A poster grid wants scanning and cross-referencing, not browsing cards or reading long copy.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is ideal for:
- venue calendars
- weekly lineups
- workshop timetables
- conference program boards
- classroom or studio rosters
- public culture schedules

It is good at **discipline, comparability, and public posting**.
It is bad for:
- deep storytelling
- heavy interaction
- dense inventory browsing
- expressive one-off hero sections without structure

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **The grid must be literal, not implied.** Strong vertical and horizontal divisions should organize all major information.
2. **Time or sequence must be the main organizing principle.** The user should immediately understand how to scan across days, slots, tracks, or rooms.
3. **Poster height and compression must remain.** The page should feel like a single board packed with useful listings, not spacious website sections.
4. **Typography should alternate between big anchors and compact schedule text.** Large headers frame the system; small entries do the operational work.
5. **Decorative imagery must stay secondary to the timetable structure.** The grid is the hero.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Night Cycle Board** — a venue programme poster for a three-day club and workshop series, showing rooms, time slots, hosts, and after-hours notes in one rigid public timetable.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **public schedule board** before anything else.
- Grid lines are structurally necessary, not ornamental.
- Users can infer how to scan time/track information at a glance.
- The composition still feels like a poster rather than a web app.
- Density feels purposeful and organized.
- The translated concept plausibly serves a cultural or educational programme.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the layout relaxes into generic stacked website sections.
- **Fail if** schedule data is not clearly bound to the grid.
- **Fail if** decorative imagery overwhelms the timetable logic.

- **Pass if** the user immediately understands it as a posted programme board.
- **Pass if** the grid itself carries most of the design identity.
- **Pass if** the implementation feels like something that could be printed and pinned up.
