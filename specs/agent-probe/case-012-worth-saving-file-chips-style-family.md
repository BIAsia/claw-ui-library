# Agent Probe / Case 012 / Worth Saving File Chips — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 012 should become a curation-and-collection utility: a saveboard for references, files, links, and notable fragments where chip-like objects act as the primary interaction model rather than full document cards or feed posts.**

This is a lightweight save system.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title points directly to **saving** and **file chips**, implying compact object tokens over large cards.
- The source likely privileges **small, collectible units** that can be grouped, tagged, and scanned quickly.
- “Worth saving” suggests **editorial curation**, not raw file management.
- Chip-based layouts are ideal for **quick triage, grouping, and resurfacing**.
- The visual identity likely comes from repetition and tag-like density rather than deep content previews.

---

## Style-family thesis
Use this family for:
- research saveboards
- inspiration lockers
- evidence collections
- reference triage tools
- creative bookmarking surfaces

It is good at:
- compactness
- quick classification
- collecting lots of small references
- low-friction review

It is bad for:
- full reading experiences
- narrative storytelling
- rich media presentation
- transactional ecommerce

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Chip-scale objects must dominate.** Saved items should appear as compressed tokens, pills, mini-files, or small labeled capsules.
2. **The page must support quantity without heaviness.** Many items should fit comfortably without turning into a cluttered file explorer.
3. **Metadata must be tiny and useful.** Type, source, tag, date, or owner should appear in short fragments only.
4. **Grouping and filtering must feel effortless.** Sections, clusters, or tag rows should help users reassemble meaning from many chips.
5. **The visual tone should feel selective, not administrative.** This is a curated save surface, not enterprise document management.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Keep Shelf** — a creative research saveboard where designers pin snippets, PDFs, screenshots, and quotes as compact chips grouped by project thread.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **saveboard / curated locker**, not normal drive UI.
- Chip-like objects are the primary visual units.
- A high item count feels manageable.
- Metadata is compressed and secondary.
- Grouping logic is visible and useful.
- The concept plausibly helps someone collect references worth returning to.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** saved items are expanded into big feed cards.
- **Fail if** the layout feels like generic cloud storage.
- **Fail if** filtering/grouping is absent or visually weak.

- **Pass if** the page feels like a smart shelf of compact saved fragments.
- **Pass if** quantity feels intentional rather than messy.
- **Pass if** users can imagine quickly skimming and resurfacing useful references.
