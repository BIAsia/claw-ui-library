# Agent Probe / Case 017 / Circuit Identity Cards — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 017 should become an identity-system card deck: a member, speaker, device, or contributor registry where repeated cards express a coherent technical brand language rather than a generic profile directory.**

This is a branded identity stack.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title combines **circuit** and **identity cards**, implying a technical motif translated into repeated ID modules.
- Card repetition naturally supports **people, devices, sessions, or credentials**.
- “Circuit” suggests linework, traces, or electronic structure that can become a distinctive system aesthetic.
- The likely composition privileges **consistent card anatomy** over freeform content blocks.
- This mode is ideal when identity needs to feel **networked, structured, and branded**.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family fits:
- member directories
- conference speaker IDs
- device passports
- contributor registries
- access badge systems
- network participant cards

It is good at:
- repeated identity modules
- coherent brand systems
- compact attribute display
- collectible or badge-like presence

It is bad for:
- long biographies
- article reading
- analytics dashboards
- campaign-style persuasion

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Repeated cards must form the system backbone.** Each identity unit should share a strict anatomy.
2. **Circuit-like structure must influence the design.** Use linework, connectors, trace patterns, or technical routing cues meaningfully.
3. **Identity fields must stay compact and standardized.** Name, role, code, network, status, or access tier should appear as concise fields.
4. **The cards should feel collectible and machine-classified.** Not casual social profiles.
5. **The page must preserve a sense of technical brand coherence.** Every card should look like it belongs to the same engineered universe.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Signal Pass Registry** — a conference identity wall showing speakers and builders as circuit-coded pass cards with role, session track, and access tier.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as a **technical identity system**, not generic profile grid.
- Cards are visually consistent and clearly structured.
- Circuit motifs feel structural rather than ornamental.
- Identity fields are compact and easy to compare.
- The collection feels branded and collectible.
- The concept plausibly supports a real participant or access system.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page becomes a normal team directory with avatars.
- **Fail if** the circuit theme is pasted on superficially.
- **Fail if** card anatomy varies too much between items.

- **Pass if** each card feels like a technical credential object.
- **Pass if** the system identity is obvious even from a distance.
- **Pass if** a reviewer could imagine the design extending to badges, passes, or device IDs.
