# Agent Probe / Case 011 / Won Soju Section Bands — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 011 should become a flavor-story product campaign page: a section-banded launch surface for a beverage line, capsule collection, or sensory brand narrative rather than a standard ecommerce catalog.**

This is an atmospheric sequence of branded bands.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title suggests **horizontal bands or stacked sections**, which naturally support a paced storytelling flow.
- “Won Soju” implies a lifestyle beverage context where **mood, taste, and cultural cues** matter.
- The source likely uses repeated color fields or full-width segments as the main grammar.
- Banding supports **edition, flavor, ingredient, ritual, and campaign message** storytelling well.
- This mode is stronger as a narrative launch page than as a store grid.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is suited to:
- beverage launches
- fashion capsule reveals
- fragrance or beauty stories
- sensory campaign pages
- branded cultural collaborations

It is good at:
- pacing
- mood segmentation
- product mythology
- scroll-based progression

It is bad for:
- dense product comparison
- internal tool interfaces
- archive browsing
- long article reading

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **Horizontal section bands must be the main compositional rule.** Each band should feel like a distinct chapter or flavor note.
2. **Color or tonal shifts between bands should matter.** The transitions should create cadence, not random section breaks.
3. **Each band must carry one dominant message.** Product name, flavor note, ingredient, ritual, or campaign line — not multiple competing goals.
4. **Imagery and typography should feel sensorial.** The page should suggest taste, texture, or lifestyle through restraint rather than overcrowding.
5. **Commerce utility must remain secondary.** A buy or learn-more path may exist, but the experience should still feel campaign-led.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Night Pour Editions** — a beverage capsule campaign page that reveals three limited bottles through stacked story bands: origin, tasting note, ritual, and drop timing.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The first read is **branded campaign sequence**, not catalog.
- Section bands clearly organize the page rhythm.
- Each band has a focused content role.
- The mood feels sensorial and premium.
- The product story progresses as the user scans down the page.
- Any commerce CTA stays subordinate to the storytelling structure.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page becomes a generic hero plus product cards.
- **Fail if** the section bands feel interchangeable or unnecessary.
- **Fail if** copy becomes too long for the banded format.

- **Pass if** the user experiences the page as a sequence of branded moods.
- **Pass if** the section divisions create strong narrative pacing.
- **Pass if** the campaign feels plausible for a beverage or sensory product line.
