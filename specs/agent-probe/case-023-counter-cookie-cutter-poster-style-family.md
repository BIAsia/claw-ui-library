# Agent Probe / Case 023 / Counter Cookie-Cutter Poster — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 023 should become an anti-template manifesto poster: a provocative publication or event announcement for design critique, craft advocacy, or originality campaigns rather than a neutral informational page.**

This is a polemical poster object.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title explicitly invokes **cookie-cutter**, which implies critique of sameness and templated production.
- Poster framing suggests a **public argument** rather than a quiet editorial essay.
- The GIF source hints that motion or repetition may reinforce the theme of sameness versus disruption.
- This case wants a concept with **opinion and edge**, not broad market friendliness.
- The likely graphic language depends on **contrast, intervention, and declarative messaging**.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family suits:
- anti-template campaigns
- design criticism events
- experimental conference posters
- manifestos for originality
- studio statements against homogenization

It is good at:
- provocation
- public rhetoric
- memorable critique
- limited but forceful interaction

It is bad for:
- neutral utility flows
- dense content systems
- transactional product pages
- calm informational dashboards

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **A critical thesis must dominate the page.** The user should feel an argument being made, not just information being posted.
2. **Repetition versus disruption should be visible.** Through motion, pattern, or layout contrast, the page should dramatize sameness being challenged.
3. **Poster boldness must remain.** Strong type, decisive spacing, and public readability are mandatory.
4. **Copy should stay sharp and compressed.** Slogans, issue lines, event facts, and one short premise are enough.
5. **The design must resist smoothing into trendy generic minimalism.** The whole point is anti-cookie-cutter stance.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Against Template Thinking** — a poster for a design symposium that critiques homogenized digital aesthetics and invites practitioners to discuss distinctive form.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The page reads as an **argumentative manifesto poster**.
- One critical message clearly leads the composition.
- Pattern/motion reinforces the anti-template theme.
- The content remains concise and public-facing.
- The visual language feels opinionated and distinct.
- The concept plausibly fits a real event or publication.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the poster itself feels generic or trend-compliant.
- **Fail if** the critical thesis is too vague to matter.
- **Fail if** motion or repetition is decorative rather than thematic.

- **Pass if** the page feels like a public critique of sameness.
- **Pass if** a reviewer can sense tension between repetition and rupture.
- **Pass if** the concept feels appropriately sharp for design discourse.
