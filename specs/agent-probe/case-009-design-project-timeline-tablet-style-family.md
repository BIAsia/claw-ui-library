# Agent Probe / Case 009 / Design Project Timeline Tablet — Style-Family Spec

## Product role decision
**Case 009 should become a project chronology workspace: a phase-and-milestone review surface for design teams, research programs, exhibit production, or editorial planning rather than a dashboard full of KPIs.**

This is a sequence-management view.

---

## Why this mode fits the source
- The title directly signals **timeline behavior**, which makes chronology the primary mode.
- “Tablet” suggests a **contained board-like interface** rather than an infinite desktop workspace.
- Design projects need a balance of **visual clarity and milestone structure**, which a timeline layout naturally supports.
- The source likely privileges **phases, tracks, durations, and checkpoints** more than raw numbers.
- This mode fits teams that need to understand **what happens when**, not just current status snapshots.

---

## Style-family thesis
This family is good for:
- roadmap reviews
- project phase boards
- installation planning
- editorial production calendars
- curriculum sequencing
- multi-stage client delivery plans

It is bad for:
- data-heavy dashboards
- content archives
- campaign posters
- pure document reading

The family should communicate **forward motion, dependency, and pacing**.

---

## Non-negotiable visual locks
1. **A horizontal or staged time axis must anchor the page.** The user should feel progression immediately.
2. **Milestones must be visually distinct from background tasks.** Use contrast, size, or shape to separate major checkpoints from supporting work.
3. **The layout should feel board-like and self-contained.** Avoid sprawling app chrome or sidebar-heavy shells.
4. **Phase segmentation must remain visible.** Time blocks, step groupings, or labeled lanes should structure the work.
5. **Copy must stay concise and operational.** Labels, dates, owners, and notes should be short enough for scanning.

---

## Concept probe suggestion
**Exhibit Build Timeline** — a tablet-oriented planning board for a design museum exhibition, showing research, fabrication, install, review, and press milestones across a fixed launch window.

---

## Acceptance checklist
- The first read is **timeline / project sequencing**, not generic workspace.
- Time progression is legible without reading every label.
- Important milestones stand out clearly.
- The composition feels contained and tablet-appropriate.
- Supporting details are terse and scan-friendly.
- The product concept plausibly helps a team review a live project plan.

---

## Failure tests
- **Fail if** the page becomes a KPI dashboard with charts as the main event.
- **Fail if** chronology is ambiguous or secondary.
- **Fail if** the layout feels like a generic kanban board in disguise.

- **Pass if** a reviewer can point to phases and milestones instantly.
- **Pass if** the implementation conveys pacing and sequence more than metrics.
- **Pass if** the page feels like a real review board for a creative project.
